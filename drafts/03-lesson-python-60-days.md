I committed to 60 Python projects in 60 days.

By Day 30, I wanted to quit.

Here's what almost broke me:

Day 7: I was excited. Building calculators and games felt productive.

Day 14: The projects got harder. Data structures. Algorithms. Things I'd avoided for years.

Day 21: I hit the wall. My code was ugly. My motivation was gone.

Day 30: I almost skipped a day. But I didn't.

What saved me:

1. Shipping something small every day kept the streak alive
2. Ugly code today is better than perfect code never
3. Consistency beats intensity every single time

What I learned isn't about Python.
It's about showing up when nobody's watching.

By Day 60, I had:
• 60 working projects
• Cleaner code (eventually)
• A habit that stuck

The biggest lesson?
The first 30 days are for building the habit.
The next 30 are for building the skill.
