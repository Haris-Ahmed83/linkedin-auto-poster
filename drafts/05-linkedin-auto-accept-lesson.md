I built a LinkedIn auto-accept bot. Then I deleted it.

Here's the full story:

I wanted to grow my network fast.
So I automated connection requests + welcome messages.

Day 1: 50 connections accepted. Felt great.
Day 3: 200 connections. Numbers going up.
Day 7: I realized I had 200 strangers in my network who had no idea who I was.

The problem wasn't the automation.
It was that I had nothing valuable to offer them.

So I deleted the bot and started over.

This time I did it differently:

1. Built real projects first (RAG system, freellmapi, 60 Python projects)
2. Started posting what I was actually building
3. Engaged with people who build similar things
4. Let connections happen naturally

The result:
Fewer connections. But the ones I have are genuine.
People reach out because of my work, not a bot message.

Automation can grow your numbers.
Only real work can grow your reputation.

Are you building connections or building value first?
